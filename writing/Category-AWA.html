1. Education and Learning

A. Curriculum Design & Course Requirements

  • Universities should require every student to take a variety of courses outside the student's field of study
  • A nation should require all of its students to study the same national curriculum until they enter college
  • In order to become well-rounded individuals, all college students should be required to take courses in which they read poetry, novels, mythology, and other types of imaginative literature
  • Claim: Universities should require every student to take a variety of courses outside the student's major field of study. Reason: Acquiring knowledge of various academic disciplines is the best way to become truly educated
  • Colleges and universities should require their students to spend at least one semester studying in a foreign country
  • All college and university students would benefit from spending at least one semester studying in a foreign country

B. Teaching Methods and Assessment

  • The best way to teach is to praise positive actions and ignore negative ones
  • Educators should base their assessment of students' learning not on students' grasp of facts but on the ability to explain the ideas, trends, and concepts that those facts illustrate
  • Educators should teach facts only after their students have studied the ideas, trends, and concepts that help explain those facts
  • Group assignments that students must work together to complete should replace a substantial amount of traditional lecture-based instruction
  • Teachers' salaries should be based on their students' academic performance
  • Some people believe that the most important qualities of an effective teacher are understanding and empathy. Others believe that it is more important for teachers to be rigorous and demanding

C. Student Interests and Motivation

  • Educators should find out what students want included in the curriculum and then offer it to them
  • Claim: When planning courses, educators should take into account the interests and suggestions of their students. Reason: Students are more motivated to learn when they are interested in what they are studying
  • Educators should take students' interests into account when planning the content of the courses they teach
  • Learning is primarily a matter of personal discipline; students cannot be motivated by school or college alone

D. Career-Oriented Education

  • College students should base their choice of a field of study on the availability of jobs in that field
  • College students should be encouraged to pursue subjects that interest them rather than the courses that seem most likely to lead to jobs
  • Educational institutions should actively encourage their students to choose fields of study in which jobs are plentiful
  • Educational institutions should actively encourage their students to choose fields of study that will prepare them for lucrative careers
  • Educational institutions have a responsibility to dissuade students from pursuing fields of study in which they are unlikely to succeed

E. Learning Environment and Technology

  • Students should always question what they are taught instead of accepting it passively
  • Competition for high grades seriously limits the quality of learning at all levels of education
  • Although innovations such as video, computers, and the Internet seem to offer schools improved methods for instructing students, these technologies all too often distract from real learning
  • Some people believe that the purpose of education is to free the mind and the spirit. Others believe that formal education tends to restrain our minds and spirits

F. Access and Equality in Education

  • Governments should offer college and university education free of charge to all students
  • Governments should offer a free university education to any student who has been admitted to a university but who cannot afford the tuition

2. Government, Politics, and Leadership

A. Role and Responsibilities of Government

  • The primary goal of technological advancement should be to increase people’s efficiency so that they have more leisure time
  • Nations should pass laws to preserve any remaining wilderness areas in their natural state
  • The well-being of a society is enhanced when many of its people question authority
  • Governments should place few, if any, restrictions on scientific research and development

B. Leadership and Decision-Making

  • Leaders are created by the demands that are placed on them
  • Some people believe that the most effective leaders are those who maintain a strong focus on pursuing a particular goal and refuse to be distracted by other concerns
  • Some people believe that in order to be effective, political leaders must yield to public opinion and abandon principle for the sake of compromise
  • The best leaders are those who encourage feedback from the people whom they lead

C. International Relations and Law

  • Claim: The best way to understand the character of a society is to examine the character of the men and women that the society chooses as its heroes or its role models
  • It is primarily through our identification with social groups that we define ourselves

3. Science, Technology, and Research

A. Impact on Society

  • As we acquire more knowledge, things do not become more comprehensible, but more complex and mysterious
  • Although innovations such as video, computers, and the Internet seem to offer schools improved methods for instructing students, these technologies all too often distract from real learning
  • Technology and media are making people less intelligent

B. Ethics and Regulation

  • Governments should place few, if any, restrictions on scientific research and development
  • Claim: When planning courses, educators should take into account the interests and suggestions of their students

4. Society and Culture

A. Traditions and Change

  • The greatness of individuals can be decided only by those who live after them, not by their contemporaries
  • To understand the most important characteristics of a society, one must study its major cities

B. Role Models and Influence

  • Claim: The best way to understand the character of a society is to examine the character of the men and women that the society chooses as its heroes or its role models
  • It is primarily through our identification with social groups that we define ourselves

5. Ethics, Morality, and Values

A. Justice and Fairness

  • Laws should be flexible enough to take account of various circumstances, times, and places
  • Justice requires that we take into account the motivations and circumstances behind people's actions

B. Truth and Integrity

  • Claim: Governments must ensure that their major cities receive the financial support they need in order to thrive
  • Some people believe that society should try to save every plant and animal species, despite the expense to humans in effort, time, and financial well-being

6. Human Nature, Psychology, and Identity

A. Self and Society

  • We can usually learn much more from people whose views we disagree with than from people whose views mirror our own
  • People’s behavior is largely determined by forces not of their own making

B. Ambition and Motivation

  • People who pursue their own goals are more successful than those who try to please others
  • Young people are often motivated by peer pressure rather than self-interest

7. History and the Past

  • Understanding the past is essential for solving the problems of the present
  • Studying history helps people understand the present and prepare for the future
  • There are valuable lessons to be learned from history

8. Work, Success, and Achievement

A. Definitions of Success

  • True success can be measured primarily in terms of the goals one sets for oneself
  • Young people should be encouraged to pursue long-term, realistic goals instead of immediate fame
  • Success in creative fields comes from hard work and perseverance, not just innate talent

B. Competition and Performance

  • Competition for high grades limits the quality of real learning
  • Teachers’ salaries should be based on students’ academic performance
  • Some people believe grade competition motivates excellence, others believe it undermines learning

9. Learning, Knowledge, and Creativity

A. Imagination vs. Experience/Knowledge

  • Imagination is a more valuable asset than experience
  • In most professions and academic fields, imagination is more important than knowledge
  • The best ideas arise from a passionate interest in commonplace things
  • Claim: In any field, a beginner is more likely to make important contributions than an expert

B. Nature of Learning

  • Critical judgment in any field has little value unless it comes from an expert
  • No field of study can advance significantly without incorporating knowledge from outside that field
  • As we acquire more knowledge, things become more complex and mysterious
  • Some people believe that disagreement causes stress and inhibits learning; others say it leads to growth

10. Environment and Sustainability

  • Nations should pass laws to preserve any remaining wilderness areas in their natural state
  • Nations should suspend government funding for the arts when significant numbers of citizens are hungry or unemployed
  • Some people claim a nation's government should preserve wilderness areas; others say they should be developed for economic gain
  • The best way to solve environmental problems caused by consumer-generated waste is for towns and cities to impose strict limits on the amount of trash accepted
  • There is little justification for society to make extraordinary efforts—especially at great cost—to save endangered species
  • Society should make efforts to save endangered species only if their extinction is the result of human activities
  • Society should try to save every plant and animal species, regardless of the cost

11. Law, Justice, and Governance

A. Law and Morality

  • Many problems of modern society cannot be solved by laws and the legal system
  • Laws should be flexible enough to take account of various circumstances, times, and places
  • Some people believe political leaders should withhold information from the public; others say the public has a right to know everything

B. Civic Engagement and Public Good

  • The well-being of a society is enhanced when many of its people question authority
  • Claim: Governments must ensure that their major cities receive the financial support they need. Reason: It is in cities that a nation's culture is preserved
  • In any field—business, politics, education—those in power should step down after five years
  • Politicians should pursue common ground and reasonable consensus rather than elusive ideals

12. Media, Communication, and Image

  • In this age of intensive media coverage, it is no longer possible for society to regard any living person as a hero
  • Creating an appealing image has become more important than the truth behind that image
  • Scandals are useful because they focus attention on problems in ways no reformer ever could
  • The best way to understand contemporary culture is to analyze the trends of its youth
  • Because people increasingly feel compelled to share their personal details online, the right to privacy is eroding

13. Arts and Aesthetics

  • In order for any work of art to have merit, it must be understandable to most people
  • Some people believe government funding for the arts is essential; others believe it threatens artistic integrity
  • Nations should suspend funding for the arts when basic needs like food and jobs are unmet

14. Miscellaneous / Cross-Disciplinary

A. Interdisciplinary Contributions

  • No field of study can advance significantly unless it incorporates knowledge and experience from outside that field
  • In any field of endeavor, it is impossible to make a significant contribution without first being strongly influenced by past achievements

B. Social Psychology and Life Perspective

  • When making decisions, it’s better to have fewer options; more options create difficulty
  • Boredom is a sign of a lack of imagination and should be a source of embarrassment
  • Reimagining challenges as growth opportunities can be helpful—or may distract from real solutions
  • Claim: People who are deeply committed to an idea or policy are also the most critical of it
